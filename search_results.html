Coaching from the inside-out Twenty years ago, fresh out of my Co-Active Training Institute coach training, I attended an executive coaching conference in New York City. I joined a session led by Kevin Cashman exploring the role of purpose and authenticity in coaching. As a fan of his book "Leadership from the Inside Out", I was eager to learn. During the session, Kevin asked for a volunteer for a coaching demonstration. Nervously, I raised my hand and, to my surprise, was chosen from a room of more than 100 experienced coaches. This turned out to be one of the most impactful conversations of my career. Kevin started by asking me about my purpose and what I wanted to achieve with my coaching. I remember saying I wanted to be like himâ€”a successful coach working with senior leaders and top teams. Kevin paused, smiled, and said, "No, that will not work at all. You need to be you." This simple but powerful message was exactly what I needed to hear in that moment. Until then, I had measured my success by emulating others. For the first time, someone guided me to find these answers within myself. We went on to explore the concepts of personal purpose, authenticity, and the power of presence. This profound experience has shaped my approach to coaching and my definition of success ever since. Kevin's book "Leadership from the Inside Out" remains one of my most-used coaching tools. For two decades, it has sat next to my desk, now so dog-eared and written in that it's hardly readable. Cashman's leadership masteries are a blueprint for extraordinary leadership and provide a roadmap for coaches working with leaders and teams. His holistic approach to leadership development integrates personal growth and leadership effectiveness, focusing on authentic, purpose-driven leadership - I can't recommend it enough
